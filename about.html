About Me

A Journey of Grace

My name is Michel Jumao-as, a name that often gets mistaken for Michael because of its spelling. What many don’t know is that my name was inspired by my father’s own name, Michael. I’ve always been a daddy’s girl, and while my father had a special soft spot for me, his love for our family was never divided. We are six in the family, and I proudly hold the place of being the second child. Today, I am 21 years old, born on October 21, 2003, in the vibrant city of Lapu-Lapu.


Growing up, I was more of an introvert. I preferred listening over talking. Things began to change when I stopped school for almost two years after finishing my first year in Bachelor of Science in Architecture. During that time, I applied for a job as a Customer Service Representative, commonly known as a call center agent. Entering that field felt like stepping into a world I was not prepared for because I was not very good at communicating with people. Still, I took the opportunity because I wanted to learn and grow. Looking back, it was one of the best decisions I made. My experience lasted for more than a year until I decided to resign and return to my studies. This time, I shifted to Bachelor of Industrial Technology major in Computer Technology. At present, I am a first-year student at Lapu-Lapu City College.


I am also a Christian who happens to be a student. Many people are not comfortable talking about religion, but I openly share my faith because Jesus Christ is my Savior and the center of my life. I go to church regularly, not because I am a good person, but because I am a sinner who always needs God’s help. It is like a sick person going to a hospital. Serving God has strengthened me, and His grace overflows in my family and my studies. I constantly ask Him for wisdom in every exam I take, believing that all wisdom comes from Him. My dream in life is to finish my degree and build a stable career in the field of technology. I want to use the skills I am learning to provide for my family and to help others in my community. I also hope to grow as a person who is responsible, disciplined, and faithful to God. With His guidance, I believe I can face any challenge and continue moving toward a better future.
